🔥 Business Opportunities
* **AI Security for AI Agents:** With OpenAI acquiring Promptfoo and Mandiant's founder launching an AI agent security startup, there's a clear and growing need for specialized security solutions to protect and secure AI agents and their development pipelines. Companies can offer tools for vulnerability scanning, access control, and threat detection specifically for AI-driven systems.
* **Zero-Day Vulnerability Mitigation for Enterprise Tech:** Google's observation that half of zero-days in 2025 targeted "buggy enterprise tech" presents an opportunity for companies offering proactive vulnerability management, patching services, or specialized security testing for enterprise software and infrastructure.
* **Data Security for Health and Government Sectors:** Breaches affecting health data (TriZetto) and government systems (FBI, Homeland Security) highlight persistent vulnerabilities. Businesses focused on highly secure data storage, compliance, and breach prevention for these sensitive sectors could find significant demand.
* **Cybersecurity Solutions for Geopolitical Conflict Zones:** The use of hacking tools by state actors in conflicts (e.g., Ukraine, Iran) suggests a need for enhanced cybersecurity resilience and specialized defense solutions for governments and critical infrastructure operators in regions prone to cyber warfare.

Watch List
* **Emergence of AI-Powered Hacking Tools:** Monitor the development and proliferation of AI tools specifically designed for offensive cyber operations. This could significantly lower the barrier to entry for sophisticated attacks and necessitate new defensive strategies.
* **Regulatory Impact on Cloud Security Acquisitions:** Keep an eye on how regulatory bodies (like the FTC, given the Google/Wiz deal) review and potentially influence large-scale cybersecurity acquisitions.
